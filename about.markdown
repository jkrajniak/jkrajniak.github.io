Hi, I'm Jakub — a software engineer and applied AI researcher with a PhD in Computer Science from KU Leuven.

I enjoy building things that sit at the intersection of engineering and research: production LLM systems, data pipelines, agentic workflows, and occasionally diving back into scientific computing and molecular simulation.

Before industry, I spent years in academia doing computational physics — designing simulation frameworks, writing C++ for HPC clusters, and publishing papers on polymer networks. That background shaped how I think about problems: measure first, keep it simple, and make it reproducible.

On this blog I write about the practical side of data engineering, cloud infrastructure, and ML systems — the kind of notes I wish I had found when solving these problems myself.

Outside of work, I maintain a few [open-source projects](https://github.com/jkrajniak) and keep an active [research collaboration](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=FUSoMAIAAAAJ&hl=en) going.
